About Me

I am a lifelong learner with a passion for teaching. My academic journey has centered on Computer Science, complemented by studies in Mathematics and Statistics, providing a strong foundation for Data Science and Artificial Intelligence. I am especially intrigued by AI systems that emulate human behavior, with a particular interest in Natural Language Processing.

Research Interests

Machine Learning with a focus on Natural Language Processing (NLP) and multimodal learning, particularly Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs). I am interested in prompt engineering and prompt tuning for improving structured information extraction, robustness, and reliability of model outputs, with a current emphasis on extracting structured data from tables in complex documents.

Bio

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Old Dominion University. My research interests include machine learning, natural language processing, and multimodal large language models, with a focus on extracting structured information from complex tables in scientific documents. My work explores prompt engineering and multimodal reasoning to improve robustness across diverse table structures.
